Joshua Ho-Sang scored the 100th goal of his career in tonight's game against the Nashville Predators on a feed by Nikita Tryamkin. The main thing is we played like a team out there, and didn't quit. We've got to keep that up if we're going to challenge this year, he declared after viewing the replay of the goal.

A native of Canada, Joshua Ho-Sang was originally drafted to the NHL by the New York Islanders in the 1st round of the 2014 entry draft. He has appeared in 428 NHL games during his career, notching 100 goals and adding 185 assists along with 322 penalty minutes. The 26 year old left wing has played 41 games with the Calgary Flames this season, totalling 11+21=32 with 22 PIM.
