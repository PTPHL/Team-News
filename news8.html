25-4-2020 NHL nominates players for NHL Awards
The NHL has released the annual list of award nominees. For each category in which the winner is voted, there are three nominees. The annual NHL Awards Ceremony is held right after the Stanley Cup Finals.

Here are the candidates for season 2019-20:

Jack Adams Award
1. CGY Head Coach, Calgary Flames
2. PHI Ray Shero, Philadelphia Flyers
3. CAR Head Coach, Carolina Hurricanes
Annual award given to the NHL coach adjudged to have contributed the most to his team's success

Calder Memorial Trophy
1. Brady Tkachuk, Winnipeg Jets
2. Julien Gauthier, Carolina Hurricanes
3. Andrei Svechnikov, Carolina Hurricanes
Annual award given to the player selected as the most proficient in his first year of competition in the National Hockey League

Hart Memorial Trophy
1. Connor McDavid, Dallas Stars
2. Alex Ovechkin, Washington Capitals
3. Mark Scheifele, Winnipeg Jets
Annual award given to the player judged to be the most valuable to his team

Lady Byng Memorial Trophy
1. Sean Monahan, Calgary Flames
2. Sam Bennett, Calgary Flames
3. Tanner Pearson, Minnesota Wild
Annual award given to the player adjudged to have exhibited the best type of sportsmanship and gentlemanly conduct combined with a high standard of playing ability

James Norris Memorial Trophy
1. Erik Karlsson, Tampa Bay Lightning
2. Shea Weber, Montreal Canadiens
3. Brent Burns, San Jose Sharks
Annual award given to the defense player who demonstrates throughout the season the greatest all-round ability in the position

Frank J. Selke Trophy
1. Sonny Milano, Columbus Blue Jackets
2. Andreas Athanasiou, Colorado Avalanche
3. Nick Foligno, Buffalo Sabres
Annual award given to the forward who best excels in the defensive aspects of the game

4-4-2020 Tom Kuhnhackl reaches career high !
Tom Kuhnhackl marked a new career high in goals with 7 tallies after scoring in tonights game against Los Angeles Kings on a feed by Markus Granlund. The main thing is we played like a team out there, and didn't quit. We've got to keep that up if we're going to challenge this year, he said as his teammates crowded around him.

A native of Germany, Tom Kuhnhackl was originally drafted to the NHL by the Pittsburgh Penguins in the 4th round of the 2010 entry draft. He has appeared in 324 NHL games during his career, notching 27 goals and adding 44 assists along with 86 penalty minutes. The 28 year old left wing has played 61 games with the Colorado Avalanche this season, totalling 7+5=12 with 12 PIM.
