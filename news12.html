Edmonton Oilers right wing Jesse Puljujärvi feels frustrated about his position in the organization. He feels he's good enough to be a franchise player for the team and wonders why the organization still considers him a minor league player.

A native of Sweden, Jesse Puljujärvi was originally drafted to the NHL by the Edmonton Oilers in the 1st round of the 2016 entry draft. He has appeared in 327 NHL games during his career, notching 90 goals and adding 134 assists along with 113 penalty minutes. The 23 year old right wing has played 82 games with the Edmonton Oilers this season, totalling 27+52=79 with 48 PIM.
